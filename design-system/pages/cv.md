# CV

## Intent

The CV page is a public professional profile for Tran Tuan Canh.

It should feel like a clear, credible resume document rather than a landing page.

## Covered Routes

- `/cv`

## Primary Actions

- understand the professional profile quickly
- scan experience and technical depth
- export the page to PDF
- navigate to external professional links

## Layout

- document-like single-column flow
- one primary resume surface is acceptable on screen, but avoid nested cards and boxed subsections
- strong section dividers and consistent vertical rhythm
- browser print/PDF should reuse the same structure and become a clean A4 document
- no glass, no chip clouds, no decorative stat blocks, no notes sidebar

## Hierarchy

- profile identity and contact line
- professional summary
- core competencies
- experience
- relevant projects
- education
- omit references from the public CV
- hide sensitive personal details such as full street address, personal phone number, and reference phone numbers

## Core Components

- resume header
- section labels
- flat experience entries
- flat project entries
- print-friendly document styling

## Motion

- restrained
- optional and secondary
- no motion that fights document reading or print layout

## Accessibility Notes

- export action must be keyboard reachable
- print mode must preserve high contrast
- do not depend on decorative imagery for meaning
- structure must remain readable without cards, chips, or glass surfaces

## Content Notes

- treat the supplied PDF resume as the factual source of truth when updating historical experience
- do not invent responsibilities, metrics, or project outcomes
- keep relevant public projects
- use business scope and tech stack, not internal repo names
- keep the tone concise and professionally credible
- prefer bullets and document rhythm over boxed presentation on both screen and print
